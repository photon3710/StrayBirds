Because the field analysis of parallel propagation of a wave along the conductor yields the same solutions and surface impedance as that of the normal incidence scenario \cite{matick1969transmission}, transmission lines where the electric field travels along it can be modeled as that a TEM wave is normally incident upon the conductor. This also applies to trilayer structures. Therefore, the surface impedance of the microstrip superconducting trilayer resonator is the same as that of the perpendicular incidence scenario. In this case, the multi layers can be treated as the cascade of many single-layer films.

The surface impedance yields the input impedance per unit area of total layers toward the direction of wave propagation. Therefore, the surface impedance $Z_s$ before a layer can be evaluated through \cite{kerr1999surface}: 

\[ Z_s = \frac{\gamma}{\sigma_s}\frac{\textup{e}^{\gamma t} + \frac{\sigma_s Z_L - \gamma}{\sigma_s Z_L + \gamma}\textup{e}^{-\gamma t}}{\textup{e}^{\gamma t} - \frac{\sigma_s Z_L - \gamma}{\sigma_s Z_L + \gamma}\textup{e}^{-\gamma t}} = R_s+jX_s, \]
